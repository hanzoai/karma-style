# Valentina, in Velvet

Velvet is not a summer fabric. That was the whole point.

Valentina began as a refusal — the idea that swimwear has to read as sport, or as
nothing at all. We wanted weight. We wanted a suit that behaves like eveningwear
and photographs like one, the pile catching light the way water does, deep-wine
going almost black in shadow and opening to garnet under a lamp.

The hardware settles it. Two gold open hearts hold the halter, cool against warm
velvet — the one bright note in an otherwise monochrome piece. Everything else is
line: criss-cross straps across the hip, a high-cut leg, and a thigh-wrap garter
that finishes the whole thing like a signature.

It is our most-requested set for a reason. Valentina is not trying to disappear.
She is dressed.
